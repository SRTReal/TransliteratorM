ConditionAl Sentences

Introduction

A Conditional Sentence consists of two parts:
  • Protasis: The Clause containing the supposition, introduced by the particle if
  • Apodasis: The Principal Clause - consequence
In Greek the Protasis is introduced by the word εἰ and in certain cases the particle ἄν is added to it forming the compound ἐάν or ἤν (contracted)

Note that in all conditional clauses the negative is μή not οὐ

Present Time

There are two kinds of sentences in the Present:
  • (A) If the Supposition is Particular i.e. refers to an act or acts occurring at one particular time or on a certain number of definite occasions, or to a permanent state of things
    E.g.
    • if he is present, he hears us
    • if you have done this six times, I am pleased
    • if he is brave, he is worthy of praise

    In (A), the Protasis is εἰ with the Present or sometimes the Perfect, Indicative

    E.g.
    • εἰ νῦν παρέστιν, ἀκούει
    • εἰ ταῦτα ἑξάκις πέπρᾱχας․ ἥδομαι
    • εἰ ἀνδρεῖος ἐστίν․ ἄξιός ἐστι ἐπαίνου

  • (B) If the Supposition is General i.e. if an indefinite number of occasions is referred to
    • E.g.
    • if ever you have money, give it to the poor

    In (B), ἐάν is used, accompanied like all companions of ἄν by the Subjunctive Mood

    E.g.
    • ἐάν ποτε χρήματα ἔχῃς, δίδως τοῖσι πενησίν

  • Note that the conditional clause is basically a temporal clause, thus the General Supposition's 'if ever' is synononymous with 'whenever'
    E.g. ἐάν χρήματα ἔχῃς = ὅταν χρήματα ἔχῃς (ὅταν means whenever)

(A) Particular Supposition Examples
  • εἰ βούλει ἀνδρήϊος δοκεῖν, μὴ φοβηθῇς ἐκεῖνον τὸν κύνα - If you wish to appear brave, don't fear that dog
  • εἰ ἐθέλεις ἐμοὶ ἀρέσκειν․ εὖ ποίησον τοὺς σοὺς δούλους - If you wish to please me, treat well your servants
  • εἰ μὴ οἱ φιλόσοφοι ταῦθ᾽ ἡμῖν δοῦναι ἐθέλουσιν․ οὐκ ἄριστοί εἰσιν ἀλλ᾽ αἴσχιστοι - If the philosophers do not wish to give these things to us, they are not the best, but the worst.

(B) General Supposition Examples
  • ἐάν τις τοιαῦτα φῇ, ἢ ψεύστης ἐστίν, ἢ οὐδὲν οἶδε περὶ τούτων - If anyone says such things, he is either a liar, or knows nothing of these matters.
  • ἐάν ἐθέλῃς τοῖσι πένησιν ἀρέσκειν․ μὴ τοὺς πλουσίους ὠφέλει - If you wish to please the poor, don't benefit the rich.
  • ἐάν τε βουλώνται․ ἐάν τε μή․ αἰεὶ αὐτοὺς ἐπαινέει - If they wish, or if they don't wish, he always praises them.


Past Time

Exactly the same distinction is found between the Particular and General suppositions in the Past tense as in the Present
  • (A) If he saw Cyrus, he killed him
  • (B) If ever he saw a soldier, he used to kill him

  • Same case with the temporal clause for the General Supposition
