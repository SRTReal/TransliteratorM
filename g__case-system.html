The Cases

Name Purpose Examples
Nominative Used for the Subject and for things which describe the subject (e.g. Predicate Nouns and Adjectives) Subject

Ἀπόλλων βέβληκεν Ἀγαμέμνονά τε καὶ τὰς Ἀχαιούς
"Apollo has struck Agamemnon and the Achaeans"

Predicate
[Recall that a Predicate Nominative occurs when there is a linking verb (copula) which connects the subject to a noun or pronoun which gives additional information on it]

Predicate Noun

ἡ Ἄρτεμις θεὴ ἐστι
"Artemis is a Goddess"

Predicate Adjective

ὁ γὰρ γίνεται πανκρατής
"For he becomes All-Powerful"
Vocative Used for Addressing people and things Address

κλῦθί μευ. ὦ σελασφόρε
"Hear me, O bringer of light (epith. of Artemis)
Accusative Direct Object. Prepositions governed by it express motion Direct Object

πόδας ὠκὺς Ἀχιλλεὺς χαλκοκορυστὴν Ἕκτορ' ἀποκτείνει
"Swift-footed Achilles killed bronze-armoured Hektor"

Extent of Space
[Extent of space meaning the way over which an action is extended]

ἀναλάβων τὴν ἵππον τὴν ἑταιρίκην ὁ πεντεκαίδεκα στάδια ἐς Βαβυλῶνα ἤλασεν
"Taking the Companion cavalry, he marched for 15 stades to Babylon"

ἄγειν στρατίην στεινὰς ὁδους
"To lead an army over narrow roads"

Duration of time

ἐλαύνουσι πέντε ἡμέρης
"They marched for 5 days"

ἔτη γεγονὼς ἑβδομήκοντα
"Having been (lived) for 70 years - lit. 70 years of age"

Of Respect
[To verbs denoting a state, or an adjective, an accusative may be used to denote a thing in respect to which a verb or adjective is limited]

Of Qualities/Attributes(Nature, Form, Size, Name, Birth etc.)

διαφέρει γύνη ἀνδρὸς τὴν φύσιν
"A woman differs from a man in nature"

ποταμός․ Κύδνος ὄνομα
"A river, Kydnos by name" - A dative may be used to the same effect

λέξον ὅστις εἶ γένος
"Tell me who you are by race- i.e. Tell me what race you are of"

πλῆθος ὡς δισχείλιοι
"About 2 thousand by number

Of the Sphere in General
δεινοὶ μάχην
"Terrible in battle"

γένεσθε τὴν διάνοιαν
"Transfer yourselves in thought

νεηνίαι τὰς ὄψεις
"Youths by appearance"
Genitive Four Primary Usages:
  1. Of Possession
  2. Of Dependence
  3. Of Separation
  4. Of the Divided Whole (Partitive)

Additional Usages
  1. Chorographic
  2. Of Measure
  3. Of Value
  4. Of Time or Place
 Of Possession
[Indicates that one noun owns another]

τὰ βιβλία τοῖο φιλοσόφοιο
"The books of the Philosopher - the Philosopher's books"

Of Dependence
[Indicates a relationship between 2 nouns that does not involve ownership]

δῶρα χρυσοῦ
"Gifts of gold"

Of the Divided Whole (Partitive)
[The Genitive may denote a whole, part of which is denoted by the noun it limits - i.e. the greater whole of the smaller part]

οἱ ἄποροι τῶν πολῑτῶν
"The needy amongst the citizens"

μέρος τι τῶν βαρβάρων
"Some part of the barbarians"

Φεραύλᾱς Πέρσης τῶν δημοτῶν
"Pheraulas, a Persian, (one) of the common people"

[When all are included there is no partition - no use of the Genitive]

οὗτοι πάντες
"All of these, all these"

τέτταρες ἡμεῖς ἦμεν
"There were four of us"

Of Separation

λύουσι τοὺς Ἑλλήνους δεσμῶν
"They free the Hellenes from fetters"

σῶσαι κακοῦ
"To save from evil"

Chorographic Genitive

τῆς Ἀττικῆς ἐς Οἰνόην
"To Oenoë in Attica"

Of Measure
[Denotes measure of space, time or degree]

ὀκτὼ σταδίων τεῖχος
"A wall eight stades long"

πέντε ἡμερῶν σῑτία
"Provisions for 5 days"

Of Value

ἱερὰ τριῶν ταλάντων
"An offering worth 3 talents"

Of Time or Place

καὶ ἡμέρης καὶ νυκτὸς
"By day and by night"

πέδιοιο διώκειν
"To chase over the plain"
Dative Four Primary Usages:
  1. Of Indirect Object
  2. Of Means or Instrument
  3. Of Possession
  4. Of Place Where (Locative)

Additional Usages
  1. Of Time
  2. Of Advantage or Disadvantage
  3. Of Value
  4. Of Time or Place
 Of Indirect Object

Κῦρος δίδωσιν αὐτῷ ἓξ μηνῶν μισθόν
"Cyrus gives (to) him pay for 6 months"

γράματτα αὐτῷ κομίζω
"I bring letters for him"

Of Means or Instrument

βάλλει με πέτρῳ
"He hits me with a rock"

ἀλώμενος νηῒ τε καὶ ἑταίροισι
"Wandering with his ship and (with) his companions"

νηυσὶν οἰχήσονται
"They shall go with their ships"

Of Possession

τῷ ἀδελφῷ υἱός
"The son of the brother"

Of Place Where (Locative)

γῇ κεῖται
"She lies on the ground"

ναίειν ὄρεσιν
"To dwell amongst the mountains"

Of Time

τῇ δευτέρᾳ
"On the Second Day"

τρίτῳ μηνί
"In the third month"

ἐπειδὴ αὐτοῖς οἱ βάρβαροι ἐκ τῆς χώρα_ς ἀπῆλθον
"After the barbarians had departed (for them, i.e. for their advantage) from the country"
Special Cases
Locative This case is generally enforced by the Dative Case, but there are some remnants of the Old PIE Locative which have separate forms

There are 2 Primary Usages:
  1. Adverbs ending in -θι
  2. Old Datives
 Adverbs ending in -θι

ἠῶθι δὲ φάνη ῥοδοδάκτυλος Ἐώς
"At dawn rosy fingered Eos appeared"

Old Datives
[These only exist for some nouns in the form of adverbs as fossilised locatives]

οἴκοι
"At home"

Ὀλυμπίασι | Πλαταιᾶσι
"At Olympus, At Plataea

πανταχῆ
"Everywhere, on all sides"

ἰσθμοῖ
"At/ On the isthmus"
Ablative This case is for the most part enforced by the Genitive Case, but there is a particular remnant of Old PIE Ablative Adverbs ending in -θεν

Κρήτηθεν
"From Crete"

χειμωνόθεν
"From the storm"
