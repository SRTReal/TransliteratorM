Verb Meaning Tense Formations Alternate Forms Examples
κραίνω to accomplish, to perform, to fulfill, to bring to pass fut. κρaνέω, aor. ἔκρηνα, pf. κέκραμαι κραιαίνω (Ep.) πολλὰ δ᾽ ἀέλπτως κραίνουσι θεοί - ' And many are the things that the Gods accomplish against our expectations (unexpectedly) '
πρήσσω to accomplish, to study, to achieve fut. πρήξω, aor. ἔπρηξα, pf. πέπρηχα πρᾱ́σσω (K.) πρᾱ́ττω (Att.) χρῆμα μὲν οὐ πρήξεις, σὺ δʼ ἐτώσια πόλλʼ ἀγορεύσεις - ' You will not accomplish anything (of substance), and you will speak many empty (vain) things '
ἀσκέω to exercise, to train, to honour/revere (divinity) fut. ἀσκήσω, aor. ἤσκησᾰ, pf. ἤσκηκᾰ / ἀσκέομαι τὴν πρὸς θεῶν ὁσιότητα - ' I practise piety before the Gods '
φοιτέω
  • (intransitive) to go back and forth, to and fro, up and down; to roam

  • (intransitive, of people) to go to or visit (a person or place) repeatedly or regularly, frequent

  • (intransitive, of goods) to come in regularly, be imported

  • (intransitive, of students) to go to or attend school

  • (intransitive, of taxes) to be collected, come in

  • (intransitive, of sexual intercourse) to go into a person
 imp. ἐφοίταον, aor. ἐφοίτησα, pf. πεφοίτηκα φοιτάω (Att.) οἳ δ᾽ αὔτως φοίτεσκον, ὅπως πλοκάμους κτενίσαιντο, εἰς Ἥρης τέμενος... - ' And they would come and go, whenever they had combed their locks of hair, to the sacred enclosure of Hera '
